About me

Welcome! I'm Funmibi Iyanda, a Nigerian surrealist artist originally from Oyo State, now based in Abuja. My work is all about exploring themes of empowerment, growth, and hope through vibrant, imaginative creations. I’ve always been drawn to the expressive world of art, even though I studied Computer Engineering at Eastern Mediterranean University in Cyprus. That technical background gave me a unique perspective, but art is where I find true joy and freedom.
I love working with both oil and acrylic paints to create surreal landscapes, animals, and female figures. My paintings often feature bright, bold colors because I believe color has the power to evoke warmth, depth, and positivity. Whether it's a figure with colorful hair and eyes or a peaceful landscape, I aim to make each piece radiate a sense of hope and wonder.
I’m a proud member of the Female Artists Association of Nigeria (FEAAN), and I’ve had the opportunity to exhibit my work at significant events like the International Women’s Day exhibition in collaboration with Nike Art Gallery and the Yiaga Art4Democracy exhibition. These exhibitions allow me to share my art with a wider audience and connect with people through meaningful visual stories.
Beyond painting, I also design handmade beaded and leather bags, as well as beaded jewelry. I love bringing a creative touch to everything I do, whether it’s on a canvas or through the accessories I craft.
My hope is that when you view my art, you feel a sense of warmth, optimism, and connection. I want my work to spark joy and offer a colorful, imaginative escape from the everyday. Thank you for taking the time to explore my world, and I hope my art leaves you with a sense of inspiration.
Feel free to reach out, and I’d love to connect!
